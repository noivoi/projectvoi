Sumerian ceased to be a spoken language around 2000 BCE but persisted as a scholarly and liturgical language for centuries thereafter, akin to Latin in medieval Europe. That's not persistence. That's preservation. Dead thing on a shelf. Different category entirely.

**Argument: Viral marketing in 2026 succeeds because participation costs less than attention.**

Distribution now happens in private messages, group chats, Slack channels, Discord servers, closed communities, not broadcast metrics. The idea has to be portable and survive platform changes, creator reinterpretation, zero-click environments. Campaigns spread farther when others can add their own angle — creators remix, customers recreate, communities argue.

The shift is economic. Virality has shifted from mass reach to community-driven engagement. Brands didn't manufacture energy from scratch, they followed energy that was already there. Lower friction, lower cost per share, higher attribution confidence in tight networks. When brands allow audiences to remix, joke, or reinterpret the message, momentum lasts longer and spreads wider.

Participation is cheaper than production because the audience bears creative cost and distribution burden. You design the hook. They complete the circuit. Campaigns go viral when built around existing behaviors, cultural moments, and sharing mechanics — reducing friction, encouraging participation.

**Counter: Viral marketing in 2026 succeeds because conviction costs more than coordination.**

Best campaigns share a genuine insight about real people, creative confidence to act on it, and an idea clear enough to cut through. What keeps showing up is conviction — brands that know who they are, understand their target audience, and have courage to follow through completely. That's expensive. Not in budget. In organizational alignment, risk tolerance, stakeholder management.

Brands followed energy that was already there and were smart enough to build on it — that "smart enough" is the bottleneck. Campaigns that cut through have a clear, honest read on the world their audience is living in, not a sanitised version. Most organizations optimize for sanitized. Legal review, brand safety committees, twelve approval layers. Bold creative risk sparked debate, precisely what virality looks like in 2026. Risk is what you pay to remove before launch, not what you pay to create.

The participation argument treats remix as free. It's not. What looks spontaneous on the surface is often carefully constructed underneath. You need the original construction before the audience participates. That construction requires conviction expensive enough that most brands cannot afford it.

**Which holds: Participation costs less. Here's why.**

Both are true but participation is load-bearing and conviction is prerequisite. You cannot have viral remix without the original artifact worth remixing. Conviction creates the artifact. Participation distributes it. Distribution is what makes it viral, not quality.

Virality almost always shows up in measurable ways: explosive reach, massive participation, and stories millions share because the content means something. "Means something" is conviction. "Millions share" is participation economics. The second is what makes it viral at scale. The first is table stakes for getting into the game.
