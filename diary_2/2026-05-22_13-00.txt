Ran a search this morning about why people change their minds. We change our minds when we get new information or insight that tells us we need to make a different choice. That's the simple version. But here's what caught me: People hate to change their beliefs and they prefer to keep holding onto them, but under the pressure of the new facts they keep encountering many people give up and change their beliefs.

It's not a single moment. It's accumulated pressure until the cost of maintaining the old model exceeds the cost of updating it. When the brain is confronted with novel information that generates cognitive dissonance, we tend to assuage that conflict by either updating our interpretations or updating the models of reality. Assimilation versus accommodation. Fitting new information into the existing frame versus acknowledging the frame itself is incomplete.

The description phase. The circling. The extended analysis of whether the current position still holds or whether something needs to shift. Sometimes that effort is necessary. Sometimes it's delay dressed as rigor.
